Setting up your placement

Your primary (first) clinical placement will start in January of Year 2 and will be set up by IATE in settings (mostly schools) that are approved to meet IATE's placement requirements.

We have many existing approved placement settings across the country and can usually find you a placement in your region (with some exceptions, where your input in the placement search will be necessary). As we have existing relationships with these placement settings, we are confident they will provide you with a supportive first placement.

If, however, you have a school or organisation in mind where you would like to do your placement, we can explore this option, but please note they will have to meet our criteria (below) and go through an approval process with IATE. You can provide details of the setting on the placement allocation form (linked below).

Our criteria for an appropriate first placement include:

  • Having an existing counselling/therapy service in place
  • A person who can act as your placement manager who is a qualified counsellor or psychotherapist, available to meet with you regularly and to complete six-monthly reports
  • Having a dedicated room for your counselling sessions to take place, that is free from disruption, with art and play materials as well as lockable storage space to store confidential artwork and records
  • Opportunity for you to meet parents at the beginning and end of the work
  • Permission for you to audio-record sessions for supervision and assessments

Placement time commitment

Sessions are 50 minutes each and you will need time either side to prepare, clear up and make notes of the sessions. Initially when you first begin your placement you will have a maximum of two clients. This will increase over time to a maximum of four or five, with the approval of your supervisor (all referrals your placement give you must be approved by your supervisor before you can work with them.) This means that initially your time commitment to your placement will likely be no more than a half day (usually a morning or afternoon) per week.

Clients

For your Diploma you need to accrue 100 hours with at least one client from each of the following age groups:

  • 3-6 (minimum one long-term client: 20+ sessions)
  • 6-10 (minimum one long-term client: 20+ sessions)
  • 10-13 (minimum one long-term client: 20+ sessions)
  • 13-18 (minimum one short-term client: 6+ sessions)

It is recommended that you avoid working with children from the same class, or with siblings. Initially it is best to avoid working with the most complex cases, e.g. children with a known high level of trauma or looked after children (your placement setting will be aware of this.) As you gain experience and increase your caseload you may be able to work with more complexity.

First and second placements

It is usual for your primary (first) placement to be in a primary school in order to commence work with the younger age groups where you need long-term work. Your secondary (second) placement might be for short-term work with teenagers in another setting, or for further long-term work in an additional school/organisation working with the younger age groups.

We advise you to wait until you have fully settled into your first placement before seeking your second placement. Ideally, you should wait until you have finished Year 2, before starting your second placement.

As you will already have experience by the time you apply for your second placement, you can find these settings yourselves, and they don't need to meet all of the above requirements. So it's OK if your second placement doesn't have an existing counselling/therapy provision.

This is not something you need to think about now. It will take time to accrue 100 hours and you are not expected or likely to have done all the hours by the end of Year 2.
