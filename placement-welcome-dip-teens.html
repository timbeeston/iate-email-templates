Setting up your placement

We have many existing approved placement settings across the country and can usually find you a placement in your region (with some exceptions, where your input in the placement search will be necessary). As we have existing relationships with these placement settings, we are confident they will provide you with a supportive first placement.

If however, you have a school or organisation in mind where you would like to do your placement, we can explore this option, but please note it will need to be a setting with no risk of dual relationships, and they will have to meet our criteria (below) and go through an approval process with IATE. You can provide details of the setting on the placement allocation form linked below.

Our criteria for an appropriate first placement include:

  • Ideally having an existing counselling/therapy service in place, or able to demonstrate a therapeutically-minded approach (e.g. with other wellbeing interventions in place and a dedicated room for your counselling work to take place)
  • A person who can act as your placement manager â€“ ideally a qualified counsellor or psychotherapist, but can be someone working in pastoral care/mental health, available to meet with you regularly and complete six-monthly reports
  • Having a dedicated room for your counselling sessions to take place, that is free from disruption, with art materials as well as lockable storage space to store confidential art work and records
  • Agreement for you to work with parental consent for all clients under 18
  • Permission for you to audio-record sessions for supervision and assessments (with consent)

Working on placement

Initially when you first begin your placement you will have a maximum of two clients. This will increase over time to a maximum of four or five, with the approval of your supervisor. All referrals your placement give you must be approved by your supervisor.

Sessions are 50 minutes each and you will need time either side to prepare, clear up and make notes of the sessions. This means that initially your time commitment to your placement will likely be no more than a half day (usually a morning or afternoon) per week.

It is recommended that you avoid working any clients who know each other well (classmates, siblings, friends).

For your Diploma you need to accrue 100 hours with clients aged 12-25. You must be able to demonstrate work with clients across this range (at least one from the low end, middle and top end).
