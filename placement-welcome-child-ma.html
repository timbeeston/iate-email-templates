Setting up your placement

Your first clinical placement for primary aged children will start in January of Year 1 and will be set up by IATE in settings (mostly schools) that are approved to meet our course placement requirements. We have many existing approved placement settings across the country and can usually find you a placement in your region (with some exceptions, where your input in the placement search will be necessary).

Please note typical travel time to an approved placement may be in the region of an hour, though we'll always strive to find you a placement within closer reach if we can. If you already work in CAMHS or another therapeutic provider, we may also be able to approve that as a placement.

If you have another placement setting in mind, there is opportunity for you to name the setting on the placement allocation form linked below. We can explore it as an option, but please note any new placement setting will need to go through an approval process with IATE, as well as meet the criteria below:

  • Has an existing counselling/therapy service in place
  • Has a person who can act as your placement manager who is a qualified counsellor or psychotherapist, available to meet with you weekly and complete an annual report
  • Has a dedicated room for your therapy sessions that is free from disruption, with art and play materials as well as lockable storage space to store confidential artwork and records
  • Opportunity for you to meet parents at least at the beginning and end of the work
  • Permission for you to audio-record sessions for supervision and assessments

Working on placement

In the first year of your placement you will have a maximum of two clients. This increases in your second year to four to six clients, with the approval of your supervisor.

Sessions are 50 minutes each and you will need time either side to prepare, clear up and make notes of the sessions. During your first year, therefore, your time commitment on placement is likely to be no more than half a day (normally a morning or afternoon) per week.

All referrals your placement offers to you, must be approved by your supervisor. It is recommended that you avoid working with children from the same class, or with siblings. You can't take on complex cases at this stage unless you have considerable experience of these client groups already, eg looked after children. Due to legislation, you are not allowed to work with adopted children.

To qualify for UKCP registration, you need to accrue 450 hours. Full details of client hours are in the handbook.
