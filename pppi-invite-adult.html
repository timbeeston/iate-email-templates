What is the PPPI?

To apply for UKCP membership, all IATE graduates must attend a Professional Profile Presentation Interview (PPPI) with an External Assessor from UKCP.

Graduates must complete their PPPI within 3 years post-graduation. The interview can only be scheduled when an applicant has completed and passed all assessments and accrued the necessary 450 client hours for UKCP registration.

The interview will last for 45 minutes and begins with the applicant discussing a profile of their work, including practice and its context. This enables the applicant to communicate about their approach and what the major influences on their own theory and practice are.

The External UKCP Assessor has the opportunity to ask a range of questions related to subjects that could include:

  • ethics
  • self-support
  • managing clinical work in organisations
  • theory
  • philosophy
  • practice

The PPPI gives applicants the opportunity to demonstrate their values, insights, motivations, clinical competencies, commitment to Continuing Professional Development and sense of meaning and purpose in a career as a psychotherapist.

The External UKCP Assessor will assess whether the applicant demonstrates they are 'fit for purpose' in this field, which will then enable the applicant to register for full clinical membership with the UKCP.

Fee

The interview fee of £{{248991518__Fee}} is due eight weeks in advance of the interview date. Organising the interviews is dependent on the number of graduates attending and availability of assessors. Payment of the fee secures an assessor and time slot for your interview.

Please note that as a result, this fee will not be refundable or transferable (except if an application for extenuating circumstances is submitted by the applicant and approved by IATE.)

Please note, due to assessor availability, PPPI places are limited and will be allocated on a first-come first-served basis. We therefore recommend you make payment for your place as soon as you are able to. The PPPI assessment takes place every 6 months (in June and December), so should you be unsuccessful in securing a place this round, you will have the opportunity to reapply in the subsequent round.

Documentation

All applicants will be required to submit their clinical log four weeks in advance of the interview date. Please note this log will need to be compiled and submitted as per our instructions, linked below in the ‘How to Apply’ section (Instructions for Applicants)

We strongly advise you to allow two months to complete your log, as compiling all the required information, and in the required order, is a very time-consuming process. (If you are unable to do so, we suggest you consider deferring your application to the next round, which will take place six months later.)

How to Apply

To apply for your PPPI assessment, you will need the following documents:
  • Instructions for Applications
  • UKCP Application Registration Form
  • Client Hours Record template

Please ensure you read the Instructions for Applications in full before you begin. This will help you in collating the documentation that is required for UKCP registration. If your submission does not meet all the criteria set out in the instructions, it may be rejected and you may have to reapply for the subsequent round of PPPIs, six months later. In this instance, as an assessor will already have been booked, fees will unfortunately not be transferable.

After you have successfully submitted your log, you will be given a time slot for your interview approximately 1-2 weeks before the interview date, and the Zoom link will be shared with you the day before the interview date.
